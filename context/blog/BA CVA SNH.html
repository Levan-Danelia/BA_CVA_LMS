<h1>Single Name Hedges (SNH)</h1>

<!-- 1. SUMMARY -->

<h2>Summary</h2>

<div class="main-intro">

<p>In the "Full Approach" of the Basic Approach for CVA (BA-CVA), the recognition of risk mitigants is a core pillar for capital optimization. <b>Single Name Hedges (SNH)</b> represent the most direct form of protection, allowing firms to offset the credit spread risk of specific counterparties using eligible instruments like Credit Default Swaps (CDS).</p>

<p>This section explores how the PRA framework recognizes these hedges, moving beyond the "gross" view of the Reduced Approach to a more nuanced, "net" calculation. We will detail the eligibility criteria, the mechanics of the \(SNH_c\) formula, and how the relationship between the hedge and the counterparty—ranging from direct matches to proxy hedges—determines the level of capital relief.</p>

</div>

<!-- 3. DEFINITION OF KEY TERMS -->

<h2>Definition of Key Terms</h2>

<div class="main-intro">

<p>Before executing the calculation, it is vital to understand the regulatory components of the Single Name Hedge. The PRA Rulebook (Chapter 4, Rule 4.7) defines the recognized value of a hedge (\(SNH_c\)) through a prescriptive formula that weights the protection by its alignment with the underlying counterparty risk.</p>

</div>

<h3>The Regulatory Formula</h3>

<p>For a specific counterparty \(c\), the aggregate recognized value of its single-name hedges is calculated as:</p>

<div class="formula-box">

\[ SNH_c = \sum_{h \in c} r_{hc} \cdot RW_h \cdot M_h^{SN} \cdot B_h^{SN} \cdot DF_h^{SN} \]

</div>

<h3>Component Breakdown</h3>

<div class="component-item">

<span class="component-name">Supervisory Correlation (\(r_{hc}\))</span>

<p>This is the "effectiveness" multiplier. It measures how closely the credit spread of the hedging instrument (\(h\)) is expected to track the credit spread of the counterparty (\(c\)). The regulator prescribes fixed levels:

<ul>

<li><b>100%</b>: The hedge references the counterparty directly (Direct Match).</li>

<li><b>80%</b>: The hedge references a legally related entity (e.g., Parent/Subsidiary).</li>

<li><b>50%</b>: The hedge references an entity sharing the same sector and region (Proxy Hedge).</li>

</ul>

</p>

</div>

<div class="component-item">

<span class="component-name">Hedge Risk Weight (\(RW_h\))</span>

<p>The supervisory risk weight assigned to the reference name of the hedge, sourced from the BA-CVA supervisory risk table. This ensures the protection is calibrated to the specific credit quality and sector of the reference entity.</p>

</div>

<div class="component-item">

<span class="component-name">Hedge Notional (\(B_h^{SN}\))</span>

<p>The nominal amount of the single-name CDS. For contingent credit default swaps, this must be adjusted to the current market value of the reference portfolio or instrument being protected.</p>

</div>

<div class="component-item">

<span class="component-name">Remaining Maturity (\(M_h^{SN}\))</span>

<p>The residual contractual life of the hedge in years. This parameter captures the time-sensitive nature of the protection, ensuring that short-dated hedges do not provide disproportionate relief against long-dated exposures.</p>

</div>

<div class="component-item">

<span class="component-name">Discount Factor (\(DF_h^{SN}\))</span>

<p>The factor used to determine the present value of the hedge, calculated using a flat 5% supervisory rate:

\[ DF_h^{SN} = \frac{1 - e^{-0.05 \cdot M_h^{SN}}}{0.05 \cdot M_h^{SN}} \]

This aligns the hedge valuation with the exposure valuation (\(DF_{NS}\)) calculated in the SCVA step.</p>

</div>

<!-- 5. RISK WEIGHTS -->

<h2>Risk Weights</h2>

<div class="main-intro">

<p>In the BA-CVA framework, <b>\(RW_h\)</b> represents the supervisory risk weight assigned to the <b>reference entity name</b> of the hedging instrument. This parameter is used to calibrate the level of protection based on the credit quality and sector of the entity being insured.</p>

<p>A core pillar of the BA-CVA is technical symmetry: <b>SCVA and SNH use identical risk weights.</b> Whether calculating the standalone risk for a counterparty or the capital relief for a hedge, firms must source weights from the same regulatory table. This ensures that if a firm buys a 100% direct-match hedge, the risk weight of the protection mirrors the risk weight of the exposure exactly.</p>

<!-- 6. MATURITY -->

<h2>Maturity</h2>

<div class="main-intro">

<p>In the Single Name Hedge framework, the parameter <b>\(M_h^{SN}\)</b> represents the residual contractual maturity of the hedging instrument. It is measured in years and determines the duration of the protection purchased.</p>

</div>

<div class="notation-box">

<p><b>Definition:</b> \(M_h^{SN}\) is the remaining maturity of a single-name eligible BA-CVA hedge. Within the regulatory calculation, it acts as a direct weight: the longer the maturity of the hedge, the greater its contribution to the protection value \(SNH_c\).</p>

</div>

<h3>The Role of Maturity in SNH</h3>

<p>The maturity of the hedge is a critical variable that ensures the time-horizon of the protection is appropriately reflected. In the BA-CVA formula, \(M_h^{SN}\) is multiplied by the hedge notional and its corresponding risk weight. This mathematical structure ensures that hedges are risk-sensitive; a 5-year CDS provides more capital-offset potential than a 1-year CDS, assuming all other factors are constant.</p>

<!-- 7. DISCOUNT FACTOR -->

<h2>Discount Factor</h2>

<div class="main-intro">

<p>The <b>Supervisory Discount Factor (\(DF_h^{SN}\))</b> is a mathematical adjustment used to determine the present value of a Single Name Hedge. Because CVA risk is a forward-looking metric, the regulator requires that both the exposure and the protection are discounted to a common reporting date.</p>

</div>

<div class="notation-box">

<p><b>Definition:</b> \(DF_h^{SN}\) is derived from its remaining maturity (\(M_h^{SN}\)) using a flat supervisory interest rate of 5%. This ensures that the valuation of the hedge is time-consistent with the exposure values used in the \(SCVA_c\) calculation.</p>

<p style="text-align: center;">

\[ DF_h^{SN} = \frac{1 - e^{-0.05 \cdot M_h^{SN}}}{0.05 \cdot M_h^{SN}} \]

</p>

</div>

<!-- 9. SNH CALCULATION -->

<h2>SNH Calculation</h2>

<div class="main-intro">

<p>With all regulatory parameters defined, we can now execute the <b>Single Name Hedge (\(SNH_c\))</b> calculation. This step determines the technical offset value that will be used to reduce the counterparty’s standalone risk in the final capital aggregation.</p>

</div>

<h3>The Calculation Process</h3>

<div class="formula-box">

\[ SNH_c = \sum_{h \in c} (r_{hc} \cdot RW_h \cdot M_h^{SN} \cdot B_h^{SN} \cdot DF_h^{SN}) \]

</div>
