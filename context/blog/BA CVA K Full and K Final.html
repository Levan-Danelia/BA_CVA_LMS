<h2>Summary</h2>

<div class="main-intro">

<p>Under the Prudential Regulation Authorityâ€™s (PRA) near-final rules in <b>PS9/24</b>, the transition to the Basic Approach for CVA (BA-CVA) introduces a rigorous, formulaic framework for capitalizing counterparty credit spread risk. This technical walkthrough explores the final stages of the regulatory methodology: <b>K Full</b> and <b>K Final</b>.</p>

<p>While the initial stages of the framework establish the gross and hedged risk views, <b>K Full</b> introduces the regulatory weighting logic and the \(\beta = 0.25\) buffer. This ensures that firms maintain a prudent capital floor regardless of hedging sophistication. Finally, <b>K Final</b> applies the <b>0.65 Discount Scalar</b>, a critical global calibration that aligns the BA-CVA results with international standards and prevents disproportionate capital impacts relative to the previous regime.</p>

<!-- SECTION: K FULL EXPLANATION -->

<h2>K Full: The Weighted Capital Charge</h2>

<div class="main-intro">

<p>In the final step of the "Full Approach" calculation, the regulator bridges the gap between the gross risk profile and the net risk profile. <b>\(K_{\text{full}}\)</b> is the weighted average of the unhedged view (\(K_{\text{reduced}}\)) and the hedged view (\(K_{\text{hedged}}\)). This blending ensures that firms receive credit for their risk management strategies while maintaining a prudent "backstop" against model limitations and basis risk.</p>

</div>

<h3>The Weighting Formula</h3>

<p>The calculation of \(K_{\text{full}}\) is governed by a fixed weighting parameter, <b>\(\beta\)</b>, which balances the two calculation paths:</p>

<div class="formula-box">

\[ K_{\text{full}} = \beta \cdot K_{\text{reduced}} + (1 - \beta) \cdot K_{\text{hedged}} \]

</div>

<h3>Understanding the \(\beta\) Parameter</h3>

<p>The \(\beta\) factor is the most critical technical calibration in the Full BA-CVA framework. According to the PRA Rulebook (and Basel 3.1 standards), this value is fixed at <b>25%</b> (\(\beta = 0.25\)).</p>

<div class="tech-section">

<span class="tech-title">The Role of \(\beta\): The Hedge Recognition Floor</span>

<p>The primary function of \(\beta\) is to act as a <span class="highlight-blue">hedge recognition floor</span>. By assigning a 25% mandatory weight to the unhedged \(K_{\text{reduced}}\), the regulator limits the maximum capital benefit a firm can derive from hedging.</p>

<p>In practice, this means that even if a firm employs a perfect hedging strategy that results in a \(K_{\text{hedged}}\) of zero, the firm is <b>still required to hold 25%</b> of the original unhedged capital charge. You cannot hedge your way to zero capital.</p>

</div>

<h3>Regulatory Objectives</h3>

<ul>

<li><b>Recognition of Basis Risk:</b> The floor acknowledges that no hedge is perfectly correlated with the exposure during extreme market stress.</li>

<li><b>Backstop Against Over-Optimization:</b> It prevents institutional over-reliance on credit derivatives to "engineer" away capital requirements.</li>

<li><b>Standardized Conservatism:</b> It acts as a safety margin against potential underestimation inherent in formulaic models with fixed correlations.</li>

</ul>

<hr>

<!-- SECTION: K FINAL EXPLANATION -->

<h2>K Final: The Bottom Line</h2>

<div class="main-intro">

<p>After navigating through standalone risk, aggregation, and hedge recognition, we arrive at the final requirement. <b>\(K_{\text{final}}\)</b> represents the actual amount of own funds an institution must hold against its CVA risk. This figure is achieved by applying a global calibration multiplier known as the <b>Discount Scalar</b>.</p>

</div>

<h3>The Final Capital Formula</h3>

<p>The total capital requirement is the product of the aggregate weighted risk output from the <b>K Full</b> calculation and the supervisory scalar:</p>

<div class="formula-box">

\[ \text{Capital Requirement} = DS_{\text{BA-CVA}} \cdot K_{\text{full}} \]

</div>

<h3>The Concept of the Discount Scalar (\(DS_{\text{BA-CVA}}\))</h3>

<p>The Discount Scalar is a mandatory multiplier that calibrates the technical output of the BA-CVA. According to the PRA Rulebook (PS9/24), this value is fixed at <b>0.65</b>.</p>

<div class="tech-section">

<span class="tech-title">Why 0.65? The Calibration Rationale</span>

<p>The application of a 0.65 multiplier effectively <span class="highlight-blue">discounts the calculated risk by 35%</span>. This serves several critical functions:</p>

<ul>

<li><b>International Alignment:</b> It aligns the UK's capital outcomes with the Basel 3.1 standards, ensuring that UK firms remain globally competitive.</li>

<li><b>Addressing Model Conservatism:</b> The BA-CVA relies on prescriptive assumptions (like the fixed 50% correlation) which can be overly conservative. The scalar adjusts the output to a more realistic risk level.</li>

<li><b>Consistency with SA-CVA:</b> The scalar ensures that firms using the formulaic BA-CVA are not punitively penalized compared to firms using the more sophisticated Standardised Approach (SA-CVA).</li>

</ul>

</div>

<h3>K Full</h3>

<p>By applying the mandatory hedge recognition floor, we calculate the weighted integration of the two risk views.</p>

<div class="calc-card">

<p><b>Regulatory Logic:</b> The \(\beta = 0.25\) constraint balances gross and net risk components.</p>

<div class="calc-formula">

\( K_{\text{full}} = \beta \cdot K_{\text{reduced}} + (1 - \beta) \cdot K_{\text{hedged}} \)

\( K_{\text{full}} = (0.25 \times 644.94) + (0.75 \times 522.53) \)

<p>The final requirement is determined by applying the global calibration scalar to the technical weighted output.</p>

<div class="calc-formula">

\( \text{Capital Requirement} = DS_{\text{BA-CVA}} \cdot K_{\text{full}} \)
