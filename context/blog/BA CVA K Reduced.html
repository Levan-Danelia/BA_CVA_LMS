<!-- K REDUCED CONCEPT & FORMULA -->

<h2>K Reduced Methodology</h2>

<div class="main-intro">

<p>The <b>K Reduced</b> calculation acts as the baseline aggregation of individual counterparty risks (SCVA) within the BA-CVA framework. By assuming an environment where no eligible hedges are recognized, it provides a transparent assessment of the portfolio's underlying credit spread risk.</p>

</div>

<div class="formula-box">

\[ K_{\text{reduced}} = \sqrt{ {\color{#3498DB} \left( \rho \cdot \sum_{c} SCVA_c \right)^2 } + {\color{#27AE60} (1 - \rho^2) \cdot \sum_{c} (SCVA_c)^2 } } \]

</div>

<div class="risk-block systemic-block">

<span class="block-title highlight-systemic">Systemic Risk Approach</span>

<p>This component captures the risk of a market-wide "spread blowout." It assumes that credit spreads across all counterparties are perfectly correlated. Because individual SCVA values are summed linearly before being squared, there is no diversification benefit hereâ€”the risk is simply cumulative.</p>

</div>

<div class="risk-block non-systemic-block">

<span class="block-title highlight-non-systemic">Non-Systemic (Idiosyncratic) Risk Approach</span>

<p>This component captures shocks unique to specific counterparties, such as name-specific downgrades or defaults. By squaring individual SCVA values before summation, the formula provides a mathematical "reward" for diversification; exposures that are not perfectly correlated tend to offset each other at the portfolio level.</p>

</div>

<hr>

<!-- THE CORRELATION PARAMETER (RHO) SECTION -->

<h2>The Correlation Parameter (\(\rho\))</h2>

<div class="main-intro">

<p>The <b>Supervisory Correlation Parameter (\(\rho\))</b> is the mathematical engine of the aggregation formula. It dictates how much weight is assigned to broad market contagion versus counterparty-specific risks. Under the PRA rules, this is a fixed parameter: <b>\(\rho = 0.5\)</b> (50%).</p>

</div>

<h3>Application: The "Weighting" Logic</h3>

<p>Because CVA capital is calculated as the square root of a variance sum, the parameter \(\rho\) and its complement \(1 - \rho^2\) serve as weights for the systemic and non-systemic components respectively.</p>

<div class="formula-box">

\[ K = \sqrt{ \underbrace{ \color{#3498DB}{\rho^2} \cdot \left( \sum SCVA \right)^2 }_{\text{Systemic Weight}} + \underbrace{ \color{#27AE60}{(1 - \rho^2)} \cdot \sum (SCVA^2) }_{\text{Non-Systemic Weight}} } \]

</div>

<div class="risk-block systemic-block">

<span class="block-title highlight-systemic">Impact on Systemic Risk</span>

<p>With \(\rho = 0.5\), the squared weight is <b>0.25</b>. This implies the regulator assumes that 25% of the total portfolio variance is driven by perfectly correlated market movements. This component captures the risk of general market contagion where spreads widen globally.</p>

</div>

<div class="risk-block non-systemic-block">

<span class="block-title highlight-non-systemic">Impact on Non-Systemic Risk</span>

<p>The non-systemic weight is calculated as \(1 - \rho^2\). At \(\rho = 0.5\), this weight is <b>0.75</b>. This assigns 75% of the variance to idiosyncratic factors. By prioritizing this component, the framework heavily incentivizes diversification; name-specific shocks can be mitigated by holding a granular portfolio, whereas systemic shocks cannot.</p>

</div>

<h3>Step 2: Aggregation Breakdown</h3>

<p>Applying the 50% correlation parameter (\(\rho = 0.5\)), we split the total portfolio risk into its two regulatory components as defined by the aggregation logic.</p>

<div class="calculation-box">

<span class="step-label">Systemic Component Calculation</span>

<p>This component captures perfectly correlated market movements (\(\rho = 0.5\)).</p>

\[ (\rho \cdot \sum SCVA)^2 = (0.5 \cdot 809.41)^2 = (404.705)^2 = \mathbf{163,786.13} \]

</div>

<div class="calculation-box">

<span class="step-label">Non-Systemic (Idiosyncratic) Component Calculation</span>

<p>This component rewards portfolio diversification by weighting individual variances (\(1 - \rho^2 = 0.75\)).</p>

\[ (1 - \rho^2) \cdot \sum (SCVA^2) = 0.75 \cdot (259.8155^2 + 14.8860^2 + 518.00^2 + 9.5807^2 + 7.1293^2) \]

\[ = 0.75 \cdot (67,504.09 + 221.59 + 268,324.00 + 91.79 + 50.83) \]

\[ = 0.75 \cdot 336,192.30 = \mathbf{252,144.23} \]

</div>

<h3>Step 3: Final Aggregation</h3>

<p>The square root of the combined variances provides the unscaled capital requirement (\(K_{\text{reduced}}\)).</p>

<div class="calculation-box">

\[ K_{\text{reduced}} = \sqrt{163,786.13 + 252,144.23} = \sqrt{415,930.36} \]

<div class="highlight-final">

Final Portfolio K Reduced = 644.93

</div>

</div>

By moving from a simple linear sum (809.41) to the risk-sensitive aggregated charge (644.93), the framework explicitly recognizes a <b>164.48 diversification benefit</b>. This highlights the regulatory objective of encouraging granular counterparty credit risk management.
